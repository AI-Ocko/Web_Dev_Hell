title: "The Woman in the Wheelchair"
number: 1
date: 2026-04-26
---

As I've made my trips to and from East Pasadena to work and home, I've grown acquainted with the many homeless, "unhoused," or whatever you'd prefer to call them, around the area.
They span a great range of characters, but one of them I took to heart, as I somehow got wrapped up in helping them find their way home.
Her name escapes me.
She was an older woman, by my guess, in her forties or fifties.
I found her stuck on the side of the curb on the cross walk, seemingly unable to move her wheelchair, which was acting as her own luggage stacking God knows how much beer, trash, and whatever else she was taking with her.
