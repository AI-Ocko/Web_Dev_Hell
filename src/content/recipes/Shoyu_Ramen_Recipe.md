## Ingredients

- 6 lb. Pork Neckbone
- 2 lb. Pork Shoulder or Pork Belly
- 1 Large Leek
- 2 Ginger Roots
- 1 Dried Kombu/Kelp
- 2 Bulbs of Garlic
- 1 1/2 tbsp. Chicken Powder
- 3/4 cup Sake
- 3/4 cup Mirin
- 5-6 Eggs

## Directions

1. Soak the Pork bone in cold water for 30 minutes to an hour
2. Discard the water, bring a pot of new water to a boil, and blanche the pork bones for 5-7 minutes
3. Drain and discard the water
4. Thoroughly wash each neck bone, and place into pot boiling water over high heat
5. Add each fragrance vegetable
    1. Sliced Ginger
    2. Leek chopped in half and cleaned
    3. 2 bulbs of garlic cut in half
    4. Dried kombu or kelp. Be sure to remove any excess white powder
    5. 1 & 1/2 tbsp. of chicken powder
6. Skim off excess material that accumulates at the top. Be sure not to stir the soup as the scum could sink back to the bottom
7. Afterwards, cover the pot, reduce to low heat, and simmer the broth for 3 hours
8. Now, place your pork shoulder or belly into the pot, and simmer for 30 - 45 minutes
9. In a separate pot: 
    1. Leftover Ginger
    2. 3/4 cup sake
    3. 3/4 cup mirin
    4. 1 & 1/2 cup soy sauce
10. Bring the tare to a boil over high heat for 5-7 minutes
11. Once both the pork shoulder/belly and tare are ready, spread the tare sparingly over the meat
12. Afterwards, cool the pork in a refrigerator 
13. Drain out the ramen broth into a bowl. It’s up to you whether you’d like to save or throw away the rest of the bones and fragrance vegetables. 
    1. Decide how much ramen you are going to serve, and keep an appropriate amount of broth simmering over the stove
14. Take out the pork. You may cut to your desire. Spread the tare over each cut tenderly and sparingly. We just want to bring out a little color in the pork. 
    1. Now’s a good time to cut those green onions
15. Bring out another bowl, fill it with water, and bring to a boil
16. Take your noodles, and prepare them according to the instructions on the bag. Be mindful of how soft or firm you want your noodles
    1. If there are any additions you’d like to make, such as eggs, now is the time to prepare them.
17. In a ramen bowl, add:
    1. 1/4 to 1/3 cup tare (depending on how salty you’d like it)
    2. 1-2 cups of broth (I usually just eyeball this
18. Add your noodles
    1. In order to lay them flat for aesthetic purposes, grab as much as you can with chopsticks, raise them up as high as you can, and fold them neatly back down
19. Add your meat
20. Add your garnish (eggs, green onions
21. Enjoy!
